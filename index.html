2

The ROI Case for Reusable Shipping Boxes

The business case for switching from single-use cardboard to reusable PP shipping boxes is straightforward — but the payback period depends on your specific volume, box cost, and return logistics model. Here's what the numbers typically look like:

Break-Even Volume

At 50+ trip cycles, a reusable PP box typically costs the equivalent of 4–8 single-use cardboard boxes per trip cycle. This means the break-even with cardboard is reached after 4–8 trips — the remaining 42–46 trips are pure saving.

Hidden Savings Not in the Unit Cost Comparison

The unit cost comparison misses several real savings that appear in P&L rather than purchasing: reduced cardboard disposal fees, elimination of box-building labour on the packing line, reduced damage claims from packaging failure in transit, and improved receiving speed at automated DCs where uniform box dimensions eliminate jams and manual interventions.

Return Logistics Design

The ROI of any reusable box programme is determined more by return logistics than by box cost. For operations where the delivery truck returns empty, adding empty boxes to the return trip costs nothing marginal. For operations using third-party freight for returns, a nestable or collapsible box design that reduces return shipment volume by 60–70% is essential.
